Get In Touch

If you’ve made it this far, we are already in friendship (or close to that) territory.

Whether it’s a project, a question, a collaboration, a book recommendation, a photo-related rabbit hole, or simply a note from one curious human to another - I’m always open to hearing from interesting people with interesting ideas.

Not every message leads to a grand alliance. Some become conversations. Some become work. Some become long email threads about cameras, books, and why modern software feels spiritually haunted. Sadly some becomes enormous regret.

All are welcome.

If You’re Reaching Out About Work

A short message works best when it includes:

  • What the project is
  • What you need from me
  • Timeline
  • Budget (or at least a range)
  • Why you think I’m a fit
  • What success looks like

This saves both of us from the ancient ritual of six emails before the actual question appears.

Frequently Asked Questions

or, “questions I can answer before we begin our email correspondence saga”

Are you available for freelance work?

Sometimes, yes.
I’m selective, mostly because time is finite and chaos is undefeated.

The best-fit work usually involves:

  • brainstorming
  • engineering systems
  • architecture and reliability
  • developer experience
  • internal tools / automation
  • AI-assisted workflows that are actually useful
  • odd hybrid projects that sit somewhere between code, systems, and ideas

If you send a brief with scope, timeline, budget, and goals, I can usually tell you pretty quickly whether it’s a fit.

Do you do consulting?

Yes, in the right shape.

That usually means helping with things like:

  • Short advisory sessions,
  • architecture discussions,
  • and longer-term engagements are all possible depending on availability.
Can I hire you for writing, editing, or content work?

In-general, No! But it can be potentially yes!

If the brief is weird in a good way, that helps.

Are you open to speaking, podcasts, interviews, or guest writing?

Yes, if the topic feels meaningful.

I am pretty introvert and my general idea is that there’s nothing in the world that I know which others do not. But when the idea is about sharing “my opinion”, “my understanding”, “my experience” - my way, rather than what things “should be” - then I’ll feel little more comfortable.

  • engineering craft
  • AI in real-world work
  • developer experience
  • philosophical engineer
  • creativity and technical life
  • books, reading, writing, and the strange overlap between code and art
  • building a life that is not entirely composed of Jira tickets

Email is best for this. Include:

  • format, audience, topic, timing, whether this is paid / unpaid, what you want me to bring to the conversation.
  • but start with “why” I am the one you thought about.
Can I use your photos?

Please ask first.

For personal or non-commercial use, I’m often open to it, especially with clear attribution and context.

For commercial use, licensing should be discussed properly like adults with calendars and invoices.

A good message includes:

  • which image(s)
  • where it will be used
  • whether it’s commercial
  • expected audience / distribution
  • whether edits/crops are planned

“Found this on the internet” is not a license model, sadly.

Can I repost your writing or quote from your blog/site?

Short quotes with attribution and a link back are usually fine. But - yeah - …please ask first.

If it’s something from a blog, essay, or long-form piece, I’d rather know where it’s traveling before it goes on tour.

Do you mentor or review resumes / portfolios / engineering profiles?

Most likely yes! But I should say the cliche - it depends!

I’m especially comfortable helping with:

  • engineering resumes
  • portfolio / personal site feedback
  • career planning

If you reach out, tell me:

  • your current level
  • what you’re aiming for
  • what kind of feedback you want
  • whether you want quick notes or a deeper review
Can I send you a random question about books, cameras, or websites?

Yes. Absolutely.

This is, frankly, one of the better uses of the internet.

Some of my favorite messages are:

  • “Which camera would you keep?”
  • “Is this photo ruined or just honest?”
Will you reply to every message?

I try to.
But I’m also a full-time human with work, family, side projects, and the usual beautifully collapsing stack of obligations.

I’m most likely to respond quickly to messages that are:

  • clear, respectful, specific, human, not copied to 47 people, trying to “circle back” before we have even formed a circle.

If I can’t help, I’ll usually try to say so directly.

Do you have a newsletter or RSS feed?

Not in a formal, polished, aggressively optimized way. Yet. Not honestly - not planning for one, as I am not very regular or predictable!

What kind of messages do you especially enjoy receiving?

These tend to make my day:

  • thoughtful feedback on something I wrote or built
  • strange but sincere collaboration ideas
  • book recommendations with reasons
  • photos with questions
  • essays or sites that feel handmade
  • “this reminded me of your work” messages
  • invitations to make something better, sharper, stranger, or more useful

Final Note

If you’re writing because something on this site resonated - a line, a photo, a page, a project, a half-finished idea pretending not to be important - then that already means the site did its job.

Write!

I’ll read!
